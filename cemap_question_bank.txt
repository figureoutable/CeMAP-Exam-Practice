# CeMAP Part 1 Practice Question Bank
200 multiple-choice questions covering the full CeMAP Part 1 syllabus (Money & Markets, Regulation, Tax & Benefits, Pensions, Protection, Mortgages, Legal/Estate Planning, Trusts, Financial Crime, SM&CR).

Format per question: number, [Category], question text, four options (A-D), correct answer, one-sentence explanation.

1. [Money & Asset Classes]
Which of the following is NOT one of the four properties of money summarised by the acronym PADS?
A. Portable
B. Acceptable
C. Durable
D. Sufficient
Answer: C
Explanation: PADS stands for Portable, Acceptable, Divisible and Sufficient; durability is not part of the acronym.

2. [Money & Asset Classes]
The "double coincidence of wants" describes a limitation of which system?
A. Fiat currency
B. Barter
C. Central Bank Digital Currency
D. The gilt market
Answer: B
Explanation: Barter only works if both parties happen to want what the other is offering, a problem money solves by acting as a universal medium of exchange.

3. [Money & Asset Classes]
Why does today's cash and digital currency hold value, given it has no intrinsic worth?
A. It is fully backed by gold reserves
B. It is fiat money, accepted because people trust governments and banks to uphold its value
C. It can always be exchanged for property
D. It is linked to a fixed exchange rate with the US dollar
Answer: B
Explanation: Modern money is fiat money, meaning it has no intrinsic value and is accepted purely because of trust in the issuing government and banking system.

4. [Money & Asset Classes]
Which function of money is described as "a common denominator against which goods and services can be valued"?
A. Store of value
B. Legal tender
C. Unit of account
D. Medium of exchange
Answer: C
Explanation: A unit of account is money's role as a common measure that allows goods and services to be priced and compared consistently.

5. [Money & Asset Classes]
In 2011, £50 in cash had the same face value ten years later but its purchasing power had fallen to roughly £39 due to inflation. If a separate sum of £200 lost purchasing power in the same proportion, what would its real purchasing power become?
A. £150
B. £156
C. £161
D. £178
Answer: B
Explanation: £39 ÷ £50 = 78%, and 78% of £200 = £156, illustrating how inflation erodes the real value of cash over time.
